History of Computers

Abacus (c. 2000 BC)

The abacus is considered one of the earliest computing tools, used for basic arithmetic calculations.

Charles Babbage's Difference Engine (1822)

Babbage's Difference Engine was an automatic mechanical calculator designed to tabulate polynomial functions.

Colossus (1943)

Colossus was the first electronic digital programmable computer, used to decrypt German messages during World War II.

ENIAC (1946)

ENIAC was the first general-purpose electronic digital computer, capable of being programmed to solve a wide range of computing problems.
